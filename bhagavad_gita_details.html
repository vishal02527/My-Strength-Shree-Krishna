Overview of the Bhagavad Gita

The Bhagavad Gita is a 700-verse Hindu scripture that is part of the Indian epic Mahabharata. It consists of a conversation between Prince Arjuna and Lord Krishna, who serves as his charioteer. In this sacred dialogue, Krishna imparts profound spiritual wisdom, addressing the dilemmas of life, duty, and the path to salvation.

Key Teachings

The Bhagavad Gita offers a wealth of teachings that can guide individuals on their spiritual journey:

  • Understanding the nature of the self (Atman)
  • The importance of duty (Dharma)
  • The paths of devotion (Bhakti), knowledge (Jnana), and selfless action (Karma)
  • The pursuit of inner peace and fulfillment
  • Realizing the oneness of all existence

  2. The Yoga of Knowledge

Important Shlokas

Chapter 2, Verse 47

Transliteration: karmanye vadhikaraste ma phaleshu kadachana |
ma karma-phala-hetur bhur ma te sango 'stv akarmani ||

Translation: "You have the right to perform your prescribed duties, but you are not entitled to the fruits of your actions. Never consider yourself the cause of the results of your activities, nor be attached to inaction."

Chapter 4, Verse 7

Transliteration: yada yada hi dharmasya glanir bhavati bharata |
abhyutthanam adharmasya tadaatmanam srijamyaham ||

Translation: "Whenever there is a decline in righteousness and an increase in unrighteousness, O Arjuna, at that time I manifest myself on earth."

Chapter 6, Verse 5

Transliteration: uddhared atmanatmanam natmanam avasadayet |
atmaiva hy atmano bandhur atmaiva ripur atmanah ||

Translation: "One must elevate, not degrade, oneself by one's own mind. The mind is the friend of the conditioned soul, and its enemy as well."
