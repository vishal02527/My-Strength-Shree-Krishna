Overview of the Mahabharata

The Mahabharata is one of the longest epic poems in the world, composed by the sage Vyasa. It narrates the historical and moral dilemmas faced by the Kuru dynasty and culminates in the great Kurukshetra War. This epic includes the Bhagavad Gita, a sacred dialogue between Prince Arjuna and Lord Krishna, emphasizing duty, righteousness, and devotion.

Key Teachings

The Mahabharata imparts numerous lessons that remain relevant throughout time:

  • The importance of dharma (duty) and its complexities.
  • The consequences of one's actions and decisions.
  • The significance of truth and integrity in personal and societal contexts.
  • The role of destiny and free will in human life.
  • The transformative power of love, sacrifice, and compassion.

Books Overview

The Mahabharata is traditionally divided into 18 parvas (books), each exploring different facets of the narrative:

  1. Adipurusha Parva
  2. Sabha Parva
  3. Vana Parva
  4. Karna Parva
  5. Salya Parva
  6. Udyoga Parva
  7. Bhishma Parva
  8. Drona Parva
  9. Karna Parva
  10. Shalya Parva
  11. Anushasana Parva
  12. Ashwamedha Parva
  13. Ashramavasika Parva
  14. Parva Samhita
  15. Vana Parva
  16. Bhagavad Gita
  17. Prashna Parva
  18. Anugita Parva
  19. Svargarohanika Parva

Important Shlokas

Book 1, Adi Parva, Verse 1

Transliteration: dharma-kṣetre kuru-kṣetre samavetā yuyutsavah.

Translation: "In the land of dharma, the battlefield of Kurukshetra, gathered are the warriors who are eager to fight."

Book 2, Sabha Parva, Verse 30

Transliteration: na hi kāmaṁ kṛtaṁ puṁsāṁ

Translation: "There is no greater reward for one who acts according to their dharma than the fulfillment of their desires."

Book 6, Udyoga Parva, Verse 13

Transliteration: saṁsāraṁ kṣaṇikāṁ bhāvaṁ

Translation: "Life is momentary, and thus the wise must act in accordance with their dharma, for it defines their existence."
