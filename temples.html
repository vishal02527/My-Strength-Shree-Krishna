Sri Krishna Temple, Guruvayur, India

  • Guruvayur, Kerala, India
  • Facts:
  • One of the most famous Krishna temples in India.
  • The temple is often referred to as "Bhooloka Vaikunta" (the abode of Vishnu on Earth).
  • The central deity is a four-armed Krishna, holding the conch, discus, mace, and lotus.
  • Known for its elephant sanctuary where temple elephants are kept.

Radha Madhav Dham, Austin, USA

  • Austin, Texas, USA
  • Facts:
  • One of the largest Hindu temples in North America.
  • Built in 1990, it serves as a spiritual center for devotees of Krishna in the U.S.
  • The temple's architecture resembles ancient Hindu temples in India.
  • Hosts cultural festivals, yoga retreats, and religious events regularly.

Krishna Janmabhoomi Temple, Mathura, India

  • Mathura, Uttar Pradesh, India
  • Facts:
  • The birthplace of Lord Krishna, according to Hindu mythology.
  • The temple is located in Mathura, which is considered one of the seven sacred cities in Hinduism.
  • It has a historical significance dating back to ancient times.

New Govardhan Krishna Temple, New South Wales, Australia

  • Eungella, New South Wales, Australia
  • Facts:
  • Surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes and farmlands.
  • This temple is part of a larger eco-village and community that follows sustainable living practices.
  • Offers vegetarian meals, spiritual retreats, and educational programs.
  • Known for the Govardhan Hill replica within the premises.

Shri Krishna Mandir, Singapore

  • Waterloo Street, Singapore
  • Facts:
  • One of the oldest Hindu temples in Singapore, built in 1870.
  • Known for its intricate carvings and peaceful atmosphere.
  • Popular among locals and tourists alike for spiritual activities.
  • Hosts cultural events that preserve Indian traditions.

Radha Krishna Temple, London, United Kingdom

  • Soho Street, London, UK
  • Facts:
  • The first ISKCON temple established in the western world in 1969.
  • TAttracts thousands of visitors during Janmashtami celebrations.
  • Plays a significant role in promoting Indian culture and spiritual practices in the UK.
  • The temple regularly hosts yoga classes, meditation, and vegetarian feasts.

Krishna Balaram Mandir, Vrindavan, India

  • Vrindavan, Uttar Pradesh, India
  • Facts:
  • Built by the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON).
  • This temple is dedicated to both Krishna and his elder brother Balarama.
  • Vrindavan is the place where Krishna is believed to have spent his childhood.
  • Famous for vibrant festivals like Janmashtami and Holi.
