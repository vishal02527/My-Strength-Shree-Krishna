Prasadam Recipes for Lord Krishna

Kheer

A delicious sweet rice pudding offered to Lord Krishna.

Puri

Deep-fried golden bread offered as a bhog.

Halwa

A rich, sweet dish made from semolina and sugar.

Ladoo

Traditional Indian sweet, made from flour, ghee, and sugar.

Chappan Bhog

A grand feast consisting of 56 varieties of food items offered to Lord Krishna.

Panchamrit

A holy mixture of five sacred ingredients used in offerings.

Makhan Mishri

Sweet butter and sugar offering, Lord Krishna's favorite.

Rajgira Ladoo

Puffed amaranth seeds bound with jaggery, a healthy prasadam choice.

Dahi Bhalla

A savory yogurt-based dish, offered as a cooling prasadam.

Charnamrit

A sacred mixture of water, milk, ghee, honey, and yogurt, offered to the deity.

Sabudana Khichdi

A light, savory dish made with sago (tapioca pearls), peanuts, and spices.

Shrikhand

A creamy and sweet yogurt-based dish flavored with saffron and cardamom.

Mohan Bhog

A fragrant wheat flour pudding offered to Lord Krishna.

Thekua

A crunchy, deep-fried sweet made from wheat flour, sugar, and ghee.

Besan Barfi

A sweet dish made from gram flour, ghee, and sugar.

Tilgul

A traditional sweet made from sesame seeds and jaggery, offered during Makar Sankranti.

Peda

Small, soft milk-based sweets, flavored with cardamom and sometimes garnished with pistachios.

Banana Chips

Crispy fried banana slices offered as a savory prasadam.

Malpua

A sweet, deep-fried pancake made from flour and milk, soaked in sugar syrup.

Badam Halwa

A rich halwa made from ground almonds, ghee, and sugar.
